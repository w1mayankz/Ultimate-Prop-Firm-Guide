The Ultimate Guide to Passing Prop Firms

Most traders treat prop challenges like a lottery. The top 1% treat it like a risk management business. Here is your blueprint.

Step 01

Vet Your Partner

The futures space is tighter than Forex, but bad actors exist. [span_0](start_span)[span_1](start_span)A cheap evaluation is worthless if they don't pay out[span_0](end_span)[span_1](end_span).

  • Look for proven payout receipts.
  • Check for reasonable drawdown rules.
    • [span_2](start_span)
  • Trusted: Topstep, Apex, Leeloo, Bulenox[span_2](end_span).
Step 02

The "Fine Print" Filter

Prioritize Static Drawdown over Trailing Drawdown. [span_3](start_span)Trailing drawdowns that reset intraday are often designed to make you fail[span_3](end_span).

Warning: Avoid overly aggressive intraday trailing rules.

Step 03

The 0.7% Risk Rule

Stop sizing based on the "marketing" number ($50k). You must calculate risk based on account size to survive volatility.

Account Size
$50,000
Max Risk Per Trade
$350 (0.7%)

*Risking $350 gives you roughly 7 points on ES or 17 points on NQ (1 contract).

Step 04

Strategic Diversification

Don't put all your energy into one massive $150k account. Stack smaller accounts instead.

    [span_4](start_span)
  • Buy 3x $50k accounts instead of 1x $150k[span_4](end_span).
  • Pass just 1 or 2, and your fees are covered.
  • Use trade copiers to scale without extra mental stress.
Step 05

Secure the Bag

Greed kills more accounts than bad strategy. [span_5](start_span)The goal is to drain the firm, not hold for a high score[span_5](end_span).

The Protocol:

  1. Target small payouts ($1k - $3k).
  2. Stop trading immediately once you hit the threshold.
  3. Withdraw and rotate to a fresh account.
Step 06

Run It Like a CEO

If you aren't tracking the numbers, you are just donating to the firm owner. [span_6](start_span)Create a spreadsheet tracking Fees vs. Payouts to calculate your true ROI[span_6](end_span).

Step 07

Mental Armor

Your strategy gets you into the trade, but your psychology keeps you in the game.

No revenge trading after a bad beat.

Accept drawdown as the cost of business[span_7](end_span).
