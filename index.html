The Ultimate Guide to Passing Prop Firms.

Most traders treat prop challenges like a lottery. The top 1% treat it like a risk management business. Here is your structured blueprint.

Vet Your Partner

The futures trading space is significantly smaller than the Forex or CFD market, but that does not mean every firm is safe. You must be extremely selective because you need a firm that pays out reliably.

Do not simply sign up for the firm offering the biggest discount. A cheap evaluation is useless if the firm does not actually pay its traders.

Real Payout History: Confirm the firm has a proven track record of sending payouts.
Clear Rules: Read the fine print regarding drawdowns (Daily vs Overall).
Trusted Firms: Topstep, Apex Trader Funding, Leeloo, Earn2Trade, Bulenox.

The Right Evaluation

Most futures firms operate using a 1-step or 2-step evaluation process. Look for profit targets between 6% and 10%. More importantly, prioritize firms that offer a Static Drawdown.

Warning: Avoid firms with overly aggressive intraday trailing drawdowns. These are specifically designed to make you fail by tightening your room to trade while in a winning position.

Mathematical Risk

This is where most traders fail. In futures, a single contract can swing $500 to $1,000 in minutes. If you trade too large, you hit your drawdown limit instantly.

The 0.7% Rule

Never risk more than 0.7% of your total account size on any single trade.

Account Size $50,000
Max Risk $350

The Math: Risking $350 equates to roughly 7 ticks on ES or 17 ticks on NQ (1 contract). Trade 1-2 contracts max until consistent.

Strategic Diversification

Jumping straight into a massive $150,000 evaluation is poor risk management. It is a smarter business decision to buy several smaller accounts instead.

  • 1. Buy Multiple Small Accounts: Purchase three $50,000 accounts instead of one large one.
  • 2. Cover Your Costs: Passing just one or two covers the evaluation fees for all of them.
  • 3. Use Trade Copiers: Execute your strategy on one account and replicate it across others to scale without stress.

Secure Your Profits

Greed is the most dangerous element in prop trading. It is common for traders to run a balance to $10,000, only to blow the account pushing for $20,000.

Target Small Amounts: Aim for $1,000 to $3,000.

Stop Trading: Once in profit, hit your threshold and stop.

Lock It In: Initiate the payout request immediately.

Rotate: Switch to a different account while waiting for the wire.

Track Metrics

Treat this as a serious business. Maintain a spreadsheet tracking Challenge Fees, Resets, and Payouts. If your payouts do not exceed fees, pause and re-evaluate your ROI.

Discipline

The market moves fast. Stick to the plan rigidly in volatility. Do not revenge trade after a bad beat. Accept drawdowns as a mathematical part of the game.
